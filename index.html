OVERVIEW

ETERNAL SEPTEMBER is an alternate reality, utopian internet circa 1999. It comprises an ever-expanding network of audio, video, and text content interwoven with its own wiki system, discrete and unconnected from the rest of the world wide web. The content of ETERNAL SEPTEMBER is developed collectively by a group of uniquely talented musicians and multimedia artists from a diverse array of stylistic backgrounds. New artists are brought into the creative process organically via existing relationships with the ETERNAL SEPTEMBER team as new content is developed. When completed, all ETERNAL SEPTEMBER content is housed at the era-specific eternal-september.net site and freely available to the public for exploration. The project was created by William Brittelle and is produced in partnership with the Metropolis Ensemble, Brown Arts Institute, and other presenters (Cincinnati Symphony, Walker Art Center), venues, music studios (Figure 8 Recordings), and record labels (New Amsterdam).

METHOD AND STRUCTURE

ETERNAL SEPTEMBER collaborators, either as individuals or in groups, create content as alternate reality versions of themselves, or "morphs" - a process reflective of the early utopian days of the internet. Morphing allows ETERNAL SEPTEMBER collaborators to create freely without IRL implications. Emerging and established artists create as equals, free to explore essential aspects of their art forbidden in their everyday careers. The content of each morph is developed collaboratively in a residency setting and presented as its own website in the ETERNAL SEPTEMBER system. From this process is born a discrete network of hyperlynked websites interconnected by an extensive wiki system and organized via an easily navigable index. The wiki functions as a pop-up lexicon unique to the ETERNAL SEPTEMBER world providing backstory for each morph and offering additional hyperlynx between websites. In essence, ETERNAL SEPTEMBER is "early internet as medium", providing both grounding and unprecedented freedom to its collaborative artists and a unique open form, user-led experience for the audience.

BACKGROUND

Throughout the late '80's and early '90's, the internet was full of possibilities - a self-governed text-based digital community with utopian aspirations and a clear code of ethics. Each September, a new crop of university students would gain access to the web, creating a brief wave of chaos and disorder. In the mid-'90's, image, audio, and, eventually, video began to populate the web. In response, AOL, Ma Bell, and other large ISP's offered unfettered web access to their trove of naive users. What followed was an endless influx of recklessness, greed, and mayhem - a "september that never ended". ETERNAL SEPTEMBER posits the discovery of an alternate reality internet free from the infinite deluge of havoc - a land of music, art, ideas and emotion where the cyber-utopian dream lives on.
